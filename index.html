Girls Scouts / Digital Experience

In 2017 I joined omission.studio, that was engaged by the Girls Scouts of America, to develop the concept, the vision and the design of the GS mobile app to be released in 2020.

I was responsible for the front end portion of the product - which included

unity and react native development.

The chosen approach was to design and organize the maximum amount of features into the mobile application in order to provide compeling experience to the generation Z user- in the form of the digital 'safe space' ecosystem.

Together with Jochen Hartmann we developed high fidelity React Native prototype.

The product included fully customizable 3d avatar system and multi-player game world developed in unity.

Microservice architecture powered by the AWS services was chosen as the Backend system.

The concept and the design -- through the use of the prototype -- was extensively tested with 3 groups of users in New York, Dallas, Washington.

User sessions validated the interface design choices, and resulted in the definition of the first release feature set.

The production team was engaged in September of 2018.

The first version of the application is on schedule to be released in March of 2019.

10 Design / Computational Design

In 2012 I participated in designing a cultural portion of chinese city - Zhuhai.

I was asked to move to Hong Kong for 6 month and consult 10 Design during design development phase of the project.

As a computational designer I created parametric models contolled by a comprehensive set of data points.

The objective was to find the optimal form for the bespoke geometrical object on the scale of architecture.

The worflow involved using object oriented programing to construct the geometrical system that produced the final form of the structure.

The designed object was realized in 2017.
